# Review

Enigma Corner (https://enigmacorner.com/) is a DFIR learning resource recommended to me by a good friend. He asked me to go through the website, complete the activities, and determine whether this is a good resource for others interested in breaking into the DFIR field, like I am. <br>

### Overview
The Enigma Corner website does something that I have not seen anywhere else in all the DFIR learning tools and resources that I have been recommended thus far, in that it manages to encompass all the various tools and artifact views that one would need to "solve" these cases into one place. From looking through various forensic artifacts to making a timeline or investigation board to their own "in-house" version of VirusTotal (specifically for the artifacts in the exercises) they truly have it all. 
Each scenario has the following: A set of case files, which contains valuable data that often answers the first couple of questions, a collection of various forensic artifacts, from emails to memory dumps and much more, a timeline which automatically updates as you locate information, an investigation board where you can draw connections between different pieces of evidence to link a suspect to a crime, a VirusTotal clone that contains valuable information on domains, ips, etc., and a set of questions for you to answer based on the information contained within everything previously mentioned. The goal of each scenario is to use the aforementioned resources to find information to answer the questions and build your case and subsequent report.

### The Report
Even though I said you need to go through and build your report on the case, it does that for you as you discover information and answer the questions. While not necessarily as useful as doing it yourself, it does give beginners a way to look at a template of sorts to see what a good report may look like, though I myself am no professional, so perhaps take that with a grain of salt. 

### The Pros
Enigma Corner does have an absolute wealth of pros, which I intend to outline in this section of my review.

    - It's highly interactive
    - It shoulders the burden of the various tools needed
    - It provides information in a concise and digestible way
    - It exposes you to various different tools and artifacts
    - It's pleasing to the eyes
    - It offers hints to guide you in the right direction
    - It offers ways to take notes and keep track of your investigation just as you would in the field
    - It builds a concise report for you to look at and understand
    - It builds a timeline of events to serve as a reference
    - It gives you information about various artifacts
    - It allows you to automatically sort anything flagged as malicious
    - It has more human-readable notes to explain what is happening in each artifact

### The Cons
While fewer in number than the pros, there are some aspects of this approach that I would consider to be cons, which I intend to outline in this section of my review.

    - The automatic highlighting makes searching too easy
    - The answers for each scenario can typically be found in the same few locations
    - Some of the questions are not worded the best and can be confusing
    - The tools do a considerable amount of work for you
    - The answers are incredibly easy to find, even in the higher difficulty scenarios
    - The automatic generation of various resources prevents you from learning how to do it yourself

### Overall Score
I give Enigma Corner a 7/10 rating. While its pros are not to be understated, the cons are more impactful and, in some cases, detrimental to the actual learning than they may initially seem. While I see the value in having these resources and skills be easily accessible to beginners looking to learn the craft, I can confidently say that as someone with marginal experience prior to completing these 9 levels (writeups completed before Enigma Corner: https://github.com/marcoshanono35-dot/Cfreds-Hacking-Case and https://github.com/marcoshanono35-dot/CyberDefenders-Reveal), they are far too easy and, at least for me, offered no real challenge that served to teach me new skills. As I mentioned in the cons section, I was able to consistently find almost all, if not all, of the answers to each scenario in the same few locations, which only furthered how little I was actually able to learn because I simply never had to expose myself to the other available artifacts. I did anyway, because I want to learn, but the fact that I didn't have to concerns me. However, it is not all bad. As I stated in the pros section, there are various aspects of the site that I liked; for instance, its centrality (all the tools in one place) and interactivity were particularly intriguing. I recognize that, while not as useful for someone like me who already has some baseline knowledge about DFIR, these aspects make it incredibly useful for those with no experience who want to break into DFIR. Overall, while I can appreciate what Enigma Corner does as a resource, I can only truly recommend it to absolute beginners looking to start their journey. While it does offer a wealth of information and opportunity to learn, it also tries too hard to cater to the absolute beginner and essentially does the searching for you. Considering that learning how to look for suspicious information is an integral part of any DFIR knowledge base, having a resource that does it for you and doesn't force you to develop that eye is ultimately harmful. For these reasons, I give Enigma Corner a 7/10, not because it isn't a good resource, but because it has the potential to be a better resource.
